The 10 Best Coworking Spaces in Bali for Remote Workers

Jake Morrison February 2025 9 min read

Bali has long been the epicenter of the digital nomad movement in Southeast Asia, and its coworking scene has matured to match. From open-air bamboo pavilions overlooking rice paddies to air-conditioned tech hubs with standing desks and podcast booths, the island now offers a coworking space for every style of remote worker.

After four years of working from Bali on and off, and testing every space I could find, I've narrowed it down to the 10 best coworking spaces on the island. This guide covers the top spots in Ubud, Canggu, and Seminyak, with honest reviews, current prices, and the WiFi speeds I actually measured myself. Whether you're a developer who needs bulletproof internet or a freelancer chasing that perfect jungle-view desk, there's a space here for you.

Why Bali for Remote Work?

Bali's appeal for remote workers goes far beyond the Instagram aesthetics. The cost of living is remarkably low by Western standards: a comfortable lifestyle including accommodation, food, coworking, and a scooter rental runs about $1,200-1,800/month. That same quality of life in London or Sydney would cost you three to four times as much. The island also sits in a convenient timezone (GMT+8) that overlaps with both Australian and European business hours, making it feasible for teams spread across multiple continents.

The nomad community here is enormous and deeply established. Unlike newer nomad destinations where you might be one of a handful of remote workers, Bali has thousands of nomads at any given time. That means meetups, networking events, mastermind groups, and co-living options are everywhere. You won't spend your first week trying to find your people; they'll find you.

Then there's the lifestyle factor that keeps people coming back. Sunrise surf sessions before work, yoga at lunch, and sunsets at a beach club in the evening. Bali's wellness culture, world-class food scene (both local warungs and international restaurants), and sheer natural beauty make it uniquely rewarding as a place to live and work. The island has its challenges, power outages, monsoon season, and increasingly congested roads among them, but for most nomads, the tradeoffs are well worth it.

Ubud: The Creative Heart

Ubud is Bali's cultural and spiritual center, nestled among terraced rice paddies and dense tropical jungle in the island's interior. It draws a different crowd than the coastal areas: writers, artists, yoga practitioners, and nomads who prefer tranquility over nightlife. The coworking scene here reflects that energy, with spaces that emphasize mindfulness, community, and connection to nature. If you're the kind of remote worker who does their best thinking surrounded by birdsong and greenery, Ubud is your place.

Outpost Ubud is the flagship location of the Outpost brand and remains one of the most polished coworking experiences in all of Bali. Set across multiple levels with views of the Campuhan river valley, it offers dedicated desks, private offices, and meeting rooms with professional-grade equipment. WiFi consistently clocks 50-80 Mbps, and they have backup power generators so outages barely register. Pricing starts at $150/month for a hot desk and $250/month for a dedicated desk. The standout feature is the on-site coliving option, so you can literally roll out of bed and be at your desk in under a minute. The community here tends to be serious professionals, founders, and remote team leads.

Hubud (a portmanteau of "hub in Ubud") is the space that arguably kicked off Bali's entire coworking movement when it opened in 2013. Housed in a stunning open-air bamboo structure, it's architecturally unique and genuinely beautiful to work in. WiFi sits around 30-50 Mbps, which is solid for most tasks but might frustrate heavy uploaders. Monthly memberships run $130-200/month depending on the plan. Hubud's real magic is its community programming: weekly skill-sharing sessions, fireside chats with entrepreneurs, and regular social events. It's the best space in Ubud for meeting people and building meaningful connections.

The Nook is Ubud's budget-friendly gem and a personal favorite. It's an open-air cafe-coworking hybrid surrounded by rice fields, and working here feels like you've stepped into a postcard. Don't let the casual vibe fool you, the WiFi hits 25-40 Mbps, there are plenty of power outlets, and the food and coffee are excellent and affordable. Day passes run just $5-8/day (included with food/drink minimums), and there's no formal monthly membership because it operates more like a work-friendly cafe. The standout feature is the setting itself: those rice paddy views are genuinely spectacular. Best for freelancers and creatives who don't need meeting rooms or phone booths.

Canggu: The Nomad Capital

If Ubud is the soul of Bali's nomad scene, Canggu is its beating heart. This once-sleepy surf village has transformed into the island's most popular area for digital nomads, with a density of coworking spaces, cafes, and co-living houses that rivals anywhere in the world. Canggu offers beach access, a thriving social scene, and an infrastructure that's increasingly geared toward remote workers. The downside? Traffic can be brutal, it's gotten noticeably more crowded in recent years, and the "authentic Bali" feel has given way to smoothie bowls and Australian brunch spots. But for many nomads, that trade-off is perfectly acceptable.

Dojo Bali is the undisputed heavyweight of Canggu's coworking scene and arguably the most famous coworking space in all of Southeast Asia. Located on the main strip in Canggu, it occupies a multi-story building with an open-air top floor, air-conditioned rooms, private offices, phone booths, a podcast studio, and an event space. WiFi is rock-solid at 70-100 Mbps with dedicated fiber lines and failover connections. Monthly hot desk memberships start at $180/month, with dedicated desks at $280/month and private offices available for teams. The standout feature is the sheer volume of community events: weekly happy hours, skill workshops, founder meetups, and surf sessions. If you want to be at the center of Bali's nomad universe, Dojo is the place.

Outpost Canggu brings the same professional standard as its Ubud sibling but in a beachside setting. It's quieter and more focused than Dojo, attracting nomads who want fewer distractions and a more work-first environment. The space features ergonomic chairs, standing desks, and excellent air conditioning (a luxury you'll learn to appreciate). WiFi runs at 50-80 Mbps with backup connectivity. Pricing is $160/month for hot desks and $260/month for dedicated desks. The standout feature is the rooftop area with ocean views, perfect for calls or creative brainstorming when you need a change of scenery.

BWork Bali is the newest addition to Canggu's coworking lineup and has quickly earned a reputation for having some of the fastest internet on the island. Located in Berawa, the slightly quieter end of Canggu, it's a sleek, modern space that feels more like a tech office in Singapore than a tropical coworking spot. WiFi consistently hits 80-120 Mbps, and the space has enterprise-grade networking equipment. Monthly memberships start at $170/month for hot desks. The standout feature is the dedicated Zoom/meeting rooms with professional lighting and soundproofing, making it the best choice for nomads who spend significant time on video calls.

Tribal Bali is a hostel-coworking hybrid that's ideal for younger nomads and those on tighter budgets. The coworking area is well-designed with a mix of indoor and outdoor spaces, and the community vibe is energetic and social. WiFi averages 30-50 Mbps, which is adequate for most tasks. Coworking access starts at just $100/month, and combined coworking-plus-accommodation packages are remarkably affordable. The standout feature is the pool, because sometimes the best thing for your productivity is a 15-minute swim break. Best for solo travelers who want a built-in social circle.

Seminyak & Beyond

While Ubud and Canggu dominate the nomad conversation, the southern areas around Seminyak, Kerobokan, and Denpasar offer their own coworking options, often at lower prices and with fewer crowds. Seminyak itself is Bali's upscale shopping and dining district, with a more polished, less backpacker-y feel than Canggu. These spaces attract a mix of local Indonesian entrepreneurs, expat business owners, and nomads who prefer a quieter, more professional environment.

Kumpul is a homegrown Indonesian coworking brand with a beautiful space in the Seminyak area. The design is warm and inviting, blending Balinese aesthetics with modern functionality. There are multiple room types including open desks, semi-private areas, and fully enclosed offices. WiFi runs at 40-60 Mbps, and the air conditioning is reliable. Monthly memberships start at $120/month, making it one of the better value propositions on the island. The standout feature is the diverse community: unlike the tourist-heavy spaces in Canggu, you'll work alongside Indonesian startup founders and local freelancers, which offers a more authentic perspective on Bali's business culture.

GoWork Sunset Road is part of a larger Indonesian coworking chain and offers a corporate-grade experience at competitive prices. Located along busy Sunset Road, it's more accessible from southern Bali and Denpasar than the Canggu/Ubud options. The space is air-conditioned, professionally managed, and well-equipped with printers, scanners, and conference rooms. WiFi is strong at 50-70 Mbps. Pricing starts at $100/month for a hot desk, making it one of the most affordable dedicated coworking spaces on the island. The standout feature is the 24/7 access, which is a rarity in Bali and a lifesaver if you work with US or European clients on odd hours.

Colony Hotel Coworking in Seminyak takes a different approach by integrating a coworking area into a boutique hotel. The result is a stylish, Instagram-worthy workspace with the bonus of hotel amenities like a pool and restaurant. WiFi averages 40-60 Mbps. Day passes start at $15/day, and monthly rates are around $180/month. The standout feature is the environment itself, it feels more like working from a luxury resort than a coworking space. Ideal for client-facing professionals or anyone who wants a premium feel without renting a private office.

How to Choose the Right Space

With ten strong options on the island, picking the right coworking space comes down to understanding your own priorities. Not every space is right for every worker, and what your favorite travel blogger raves about might not suit your actual workflow at all. Here are the key criteria to consider before committing to a monthly membership.

Internet reliability matters more than raw speed. A space advertising 100 Mbps is useless if the connection drops three times a day. Ask about backup connections, generators, and what happens during power outages (which are common in Bali). For video calls and uploads, you want at least 30 Mbps upload speed, and you want it consistently. I always test a space's WiFi during peak hours (10 AM - 2 PM) before buying a monthly plan, because off-peak speeds are misleading.

Community vs. focus is a real trade-off. Spaces like Dojo and Hubud are fantastic for networking and meeting people, but the social energy can be distracting if you need deep-focus time. Conversely, spaces like Outpost and BWork are productivity-first but can feel isolated if you're new to the island and looking to make friends. The best approach for most people: pick your space based on your primary need (work or community), and supplement with cafes or events for the other.

Price adds up quickly, so match your budget to your usage. If you're working a standard 40-hour week, a monthly membership almost always beats day passes. But if you split time between a coworking space and laptop-friendly cafes, a 10-day punch card or part-time plan (offered at most spaces) might save you money. Also factor in food and drink costs: spaces without kitchens or with expensive on-site cafes can quietly inflate your monthly budget by $100-200.

Location relative to your accommodation is underrated. Bali traffic is genuinely terrible, especially in Canggu during rush hour. A coworking space that's a "10-minute ride" can take 40 minutes at the wrong time of day. Live close to where you work, or choose a space near your home rather than the trendy one across town. Your productivity (and sanity) will thank you.

Pro Tips for Coworking in Bali

Always have backup internet. No matter how reliable your coworking space is, Bali's infrastructure can be unpredictable. Get a local SIM card with a generous data plan from Telkomsel (the most reliable network) as soon as you arrive. A 30 GB plan costs around $10-15/month and can save you during outages. I also carry a portable WiFi hotspot as a second backup for critical calls. It might sound like overkill, but when you're on a client call and the power goes out, you'll be glad you prepared.

Plan around rainy season. Bali's wet season runs roughly from November through March, and it affects more than just your beach plans. Heavy rains can cause flooding, power outages, and road closures. Coworking spaces with backup generators (Dojo, Outpost, BWork) become significantly more valuable during these months. Mornings are usually dry even in peak rainy season, so consider front-loading your most important work into the first half of the day. The upside? Rainy season means fewer tourists, lower prices, and a more relaxed vibe at every space.

Power outages are normal; prepare accordingly. Even outside rainy season, brief power cuts happen regularly in Bali. Keep your laptop charged above 50% at all times, save your work frequently, and avoid scheduling critical video calls during thunderstorms. Most professional coworking spaces have UPS (uninterruptible power supply) systems and generators, but smaller spaces and cafes usually don't. Ask about backup power before committing to a space if uptime is critical for your work.

Try before you buy. Almost every space on this list offers day passes, and you should absolutely use them. Spend a full working day at a space before committing to a monthly plan. Pay attention to the noise levels during peak hours, the chair comfort over a full day (not just 20 minutes), the actual WiFi speeds, and whether the vibe matches your work style. I've seen many nomads buy a monthly pass somewhere based on a quick tour, only to switch after a week because the reality didn't match their first impression.

Final Picks

After testing all ten spaces extensively across multiple stays in Bali, here are my top three overall recommendations for different types of remote workers:

Best Overall: Dojo Bali (Canggu). It's the complete package. The internet is fast and reliable, the space is well-designed for different work modes, the community is unmatched, and the events calendar alone is worth the membership. If you can only join one space in Bali, make it Dojo. The price ($180/month for a hot desk) is a steal for what you get. It's loud and social, which won't suit everyone, but for the majority of nomads, it's the gold standard.

Best for Productivity: BWork Bali (Canggu). If your work demands fast, rock-solid internet and minimal distractions, BWork is the one. The 80-120 Mbps speeds, professional meeting rooms, and quieter atmosphere make it ideal for developers, designers, and anyone who spends their day on video calls. It doesn't have the community energy of Dojo or Hubud, but that's precisely the point. You're here to get things done.

Best for the Experience: Hubud (Ubud). No coworking space in Bali, or perhaps the world, looks and feels quite like Hubud. Working from a handcrafted bamboo structure in the cultural heart of Bali is an experience that transcends mere productivity. The community programming is outstanding, and the nomads you'll meet here tend to be intentional, thoughtful people building interesting businesses and creative projects. If you're in Bali for the experience as much as the work, Hubud is unmissable.
