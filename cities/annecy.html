Best Neighborhoods in Annecy

Where to base yourself for the perfect nomad experience

Vieille Ville

$$$$
Picturesque & Historic

Venice of the Alps

Annecy's Old Town is a fairytale - medieval arcaded streets, pastel buildings, and canals flowing from the crystal-clear lake. The Palais de l'Île island prison is the most photographed spot in France.

Best for: Photography lovers, romantics, and those seeking French Alpine charm
✓ Pros
  • Stunning beauty
  • Lake access
  • Historic charm
  • Great restaurants
✗ Cons
  • Very touristy
  • Expensive
  • Crowded summer

Annecy-le-Vieux

$$$
Residential & Scenic

The original village above the lake

The old village sits on the hillside above modern Annecy - quieter, more residential, with spectacular lake views. It's where locals escape the tourist crowds while staying close to everything.

Best for: Long-term stays, families, and those seeking quiet with views
✓ Pros
  • Amazing views
  • Quieter
  • Local feel
  • Good schools
✗ Cons
  • Hilly walks
  • Car helpful
  • Fewer restaurants

Les Marquisats

$$$
Lakeside & Active

Annecy's lakeside leisure zone

South of the old town, Les Marquisats is where locals swim, paddleboard, and enjoy the lake. Parks, beaches, and the Impérial Palace create a perfect summer lifestyle.

Best for: Water sports enthusiasts, active lifestyles, and summer lovers
✓ Pros
  • Lake beaches
  • Water sports
  • Parks
  • Less crowded
✗ Cons
  • Far from old town
  • Seasonal
  • Limited dining

Cran-Gevrier

$$
Practical & Affordable

Affordable Annecy alternative

Just west of Annecy, Cran-Gevrier offers more affordable living while staying connected to the lake and mountains. It's more industrial but has good amenities and transport links.

Best for: Budget-conscious nomads, long-term stays, and those prioritizing value
✓ Pros
  • More affordable
  • Good transport
  • Supermarkets
  • Mountain access
✗ Cons
  • Less charming
  • Industrial areas
  • No lake frontage

Sevrier

$$$
Village & Peaceful

Quiet lakeside village life

South along the lake, Sevrier is a small commune with direct lake access - perfect for those wanting to wake up to water views without Annecy's crowds and prices.

Best for: Nature lovers, cyclists, and those seeking lakeside tranquility
✓ Pros
  • Lake frontage
  • Cycle path
  • Peaceful
  • Mountain views
✗ Cons
  • Car needed
  • Limited services
  • Very quiet
