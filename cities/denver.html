Best Neighborhoods in Denver

Where to base yourself for the perfect nomad experience

RiNo (River North Art District)

$$$
Artistic & Industrial

Denver's street art creative hub

RiNo is Denver's coolest neighborhood - warehouse murals, craft breweries, and creative energy. It's where Denver's art scene and craft beer culture collide.

Best for: Artists, beer lovers, and those seeking creative Denver
✓ Pros
  • Amazing street art
  • Craft breweries
  • Creative scene
  • Great restaurants
✗ Cons
  • Gentrifying fast
  • Industrial areas
  • Parking issues

LoDo (Lower Downtown)

$$$
Historic & Entertainment

Denver's historic entertainment heart

LoDo is Denver's original downtown - Victorian buildings, Union Station, and sports venues. It's the city's entertainment and nightlife center.

Best for: Sports fans, nightlife seekers, and those seeking central Denver
✓ Pros
  • Union Station
  • Sports venues
  • Nightlife
  • Historic buildings
✗ Cons
  • Expensive
  • Crowded game days
  • Tourist-heavy

Capitol Hill

$$
Diverse & Urban

Denver's diverse urban village

Capitol Hill is Denver's most diverse neighborhood - LGBTQ+ friendly, historic mansions, and vibrant nightlife. It's eclectic, walkable, and unapologetically urban.

Best for: LGBTQ+ travelers, young professionals, and those seeking urban Denver
✓ Pros
  • Diverse community
  • Walkable
  • LGBTQ+ friendly
  • Good nightlife
✗ Cons
  • Parking nightmare
  • Some safety concerns
  • Noisy areas

Cherry Creek

$$$$
Upscale & Shopping

Denver's upscale shopping destination

Cherry Creek is Denver's luxury district - high-end shopping, excellent restaurants, and manicured streets. It's where Denver's money lives and shops.

Best for: Shoppers, luxury travelers, and those seeking polished Denver
✓ Pros
  • Great shopping
  • Excellent restaurants
  • Safe
  • Beautiful streets
✗ Cons
  • Expensive
  • Less character
  • Suburban feel

Highlands (LoHi/Highland)

$$$
Trendy & Family

Denver's trendy neighborhood with views

The Highlands is Denver's most desirable neighborhood - mountain views, excellent restaurants, and family-friendly streets. It's where Denver's young families settle.

Best for: Families, foodies, and those seeking Denver's best living
✓ Pros
  • Mountain views
  • Great restaurants
  • Family-friendly
  • Walkable
✗ Cons
  • Very expensive
  • Crowded restaurants
  • Parking issues
