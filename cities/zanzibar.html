Best Neighborhoods in Zanzibar

Where to base yourself for the perfect nomad experience

Stone Town

$$
Historic & Labyrinthine

Zanzibar's UNESCO labyrinth heart

Stone Town is Zanzibar's historic core - UNESCO-listed maze of alleys, carved doors, and Swahili-Arab heritage. It's atmospheric, confusing, and unforgettable.

Best for: History lovers, photographers, and those seeking cultural Zanzibar
✓ Pros
  • UNESCO site
  • Historic atmosphere
  • Carved doors
  • Rooftop restaurants
✗ Cons
  • Easy to get lost
  • Touts
  • Can be hot

Nungwi

$$
Beach Party

Zanzibar's northern beach paradise

Nungwi is Zanzibar's party beach - stunning white sand, beach bars, and vibrant nightlife. It's the most developed and liveliest beach area.

Best for: Beach lovers, party seekers, and those seeking vibrant Zanzibar
✓ Pros
  • Beautiful beach
  • No tide issues
  • Nightlife
  • Water sports
✗ Cons
  • Touristy
  • Beach sellers
  • Can be rowdy

Paje

$
Active & Backpacker

Zanzibar's kitesurf and backpacker hub

Paje is Zanzibar's active beach - world-class kitesurfing, backpacker lodges, and laid-back atmosphere. The tide creates unique lagoon conditions.

Best for: Kitesurfers, backpackers, and those seeking active Zanzibar
✓ Pros
  • World-class kiting
  • Backpacker vibe
  • Affordable
  • Tide pools
✗ Cons
  • Big tide changes
  • Far from Stone Town
  • Limited luxury

Kendwa

$$$
Upscale Beach

Zanzibar's upscale beach retreat

Kendwa is Zanzibar's most upscale beach - no tide issues, resort hotels, and Full Moon parties. It's beautiful and more polished than Nungwi.

Best for: Couples, luxury travelers, and those seeking polished Zanzibar
✓ Pros
  • No tide issues
  • Beautiful beach
  • Good resorts
  • Full Moon parties
✗ Cons
  • Expensive
  • Less authentic
  • Resort-dominated

Jambiani

$
Authentic & Quiet

Zanzibar's authentic seaweed coast

Jambiani is authentic Zanzibar - seaweed farming, local villages, and quiet beaches. It's peaceful and offers genuine Zanzibari culture.

Best for: Culture seekers, peace lovers, and those seeking real Zanzibar
✓ Pros
  • Authentic culture
  • Seaweed farming
  • Quiet beaches
  • Local life
✗ Cons
  • Big tides
  • Limited nightlife
  • Basic infrastructure
