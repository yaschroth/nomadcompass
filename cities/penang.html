Best Neighborhoods in Penang

Where to base yourself for the perfect nomad experience

George Town (UNESCO Zone)

$$
Heritage & Cultural

Street art and heritage in Malaysia's cultural jewel

George Town's UNESCO zone is Southeast Asia's most photogenic neighborhood - street art, colonial shophouses, and incredible street food. Every corner reveals history, from clan jetties to Cheong Fatt Tze mansion. Cultural immersion guaranteed.

Best for: Culture lovers, photographers, and nomads seeking Malaysia's most characterful area
✓ Pros
  • Incredible heritage
  • Street art
  • Best street food
  • Walkable
✗ Cons
  • Touristy
  • Hot walking
  • Limited modern amenities

Gurney Drive

$$$
Modern & Waterfront

Seaside promenade with hawker food fame

Gurney Drive is Penang's upscale waterfront - famous hawker center, shopping malls, and sea views. High-rise condos attract expats seeking modern amenities with local food access. Penang's most comfortable living.

Best for: Expats, families, and nomads seeking modern comforts with famous food
✓ Pros
  • Famous hawkers
  • Sea views
  • Modern malls
  • Good condos
✗ Cons
  • Expensive for Penang
  • Less character
  • Need transport

Tanjung Bungah / Batu Ferringhi

$$
Beach & Resort

Beach resort living north of the city

The northern beaches offer resort-style Penang living - beach access, hotels, and tourist infrastructure. Batu Ferringhi has night markets and water sports; Tanjung Bungah is quieter. Beach life, if you don't mind the commute.

Best for: Beach lovers, families, and nomads prioritizing beach access
✓ Pros
  • Beach access
  • Resort amenities
  • Night market
  • Water sports
✗ Cons
  • Far from George Town
  • Touristy
  • Need transport

Pulau Tikus

$$
Residential & Local

Quiet residential charm between heritage and beach

Pulau Tikus is Penang's established residential area - tree-lined streets, good local food, and proximity to both George Town and Gurney Drive. Popular with long-term expats seeking neighborhood life.

Best for: Long-term expats, families, and nomads seeking quiet with access
✓ Pros
  • Quiet streets
  • Good food
  • Central location
  • Local feel
✗ Cons
  • Not exciting
  • Aging buildings
  • Limited nightlife

Jelutong / Gelugor

$
Local & Budget

Local Penang living at budget prices

Jelutong and Gelugor are where Penangites actually live - local hawker centers, affordable apartments, and genuine neighborhood life. Best value on the island, though you'll trade convenience for authenticity.

Best for: Budget nomads, long-term stayers, and those seeking authentic local life
✓ Pros
  • Best value
  • Authentic local
  • Great hawkers
  • Real Penang
✗ Cons
  • Basic infrastructure
  • Little English
  • Need transport
