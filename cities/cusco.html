Best Neighborhoods in Cusco

Where to base yourself for the perfect nomad experience

Plaza de Armas/Centro

$$$
Historic & Central

Cusco's historic Incan-Spanish heart

Plaza de Armas is Cusco's magnificent center - colonial churches on Incan foundations, bustling arcades, and tourist services. It's the essential starting point.

Best for: First-time visitors, history lovers, and those seeking central Cusco
✓ Pros
  • Historic beauty
  • All amenities
  • Great restaurants
  • Central
✗ Cons
  • Very touristy
  • Expensive
  • Aggressive touts

San Blas

$$
Bohemian & Artsy

Cusco's artsy bohemian hillside

San Blas is Cusco's artistic quarter - steep cobblestone streets, artisan workshops, and bohemian cafes. The views over the city are stunning.

Best for: Artists, photographers, and those seeking creative Cusco
✓ Pros
  • Amazing views
  • Artisan shops
  • Bohemian cafes
  • Authentic feel
✗ Cons
  • Very steep
  • Altitude challenge
  • Tourist crowds

San Cristóbal

$
Local & Panoramic

Cusco's local hilltop neighborhood

San Cristóbal sits above the tourist zone - local families, panoramic views, and authentic Cusqueño life. The hilltop church offers the best sunset views.

Best for: Photographers, budget travelers, and those seeking local atmosphere
✓ Pros
  • Best views
  • Local neighborhood
  • Affordable
  • Authentic
✗ Cons
  • Steep climb
  • Limited restaurants
  • Far from amenities

Santa Ana

$
Market & Local

Cusco's authentic market neighborhood

Santa Ana surrounds Cusco's main market - local food stalls, traditional life, and genuine Cusqueño character. It's where locals shop and eat.

Best for: Foodies, budget travelers, and those seeking authentic Cusco
✓ Pros
  • Best market
  • Authentic food
  • Local prices
  • Real Cusco
✗ Cons
  • Less polished
  • Some safety concerns
  • Limited tourism services

Lucrepata/Magisterio

$
Residential & Quiet

Cusco's quiet residential retreat

Lucrepata and Magisterio are Cusco's residential neighborhoods - quiet streets, local restaurants, and long-term expat community. Good for acclimatizing to altitude.

Best for: Long-term stayers, those acclimatizing, and seeking peace
✓ Pros
  • Quiet atmosphere
  • Lower altitude
  • Local restaurants
  • Affordable
✗ Cons
  • Far from sights
  • Limited nightlife
  • Needs transport
