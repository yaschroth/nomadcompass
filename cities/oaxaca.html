Best Neighborhoods in Oaxaca

Where to base yourself for the perfect nomad experience

Centro Histórico

$$
Colonial & Cultural

Colonial beauty in Mexico's cultural heart

Oaxaca's Centro is a UNESCO treasure - baroque churches, shaded plazas, and some of Mexico's best food. The zócalo buzzes with life, mezcal bars dot the streets, and nomads gather in colonial courtyards turned cafes. Essential Oaxaca.

Best for: Culture lovers, foodies, and nomads seeking Oaxaca's most immersive experience
✓ Pros
  • Incredible culture
  • Best restaurants
  • Colonial beauty
  • Walkable
✗ Cons
  • Touristy
  • Noisy festivals
  • Higher prices

Jalatlaco

$
Colorful & Local

Oaxaca's colorful neighborhood with local soul

Jalatlaco is Oaxaca's most charming residential neighborhood - colorful streets, local markets, and the famous bread of the same name. Just east of Centro, it offers authentic barrio life with growing cafe scene. Instagrammable and real.

Best for: Photographers, long-term nomads, and those seeking authentic neighborhood life
✓ Pros
  • Beautiful streets
  • Local character
  • Famous bread
  • Near Centro
✗ Cons
  • Limited cafes
  • Basic infrastructure
  • Noisy mornings

Reforma

$$
Upscale & Quiet

Oaxaca's upscale residential calm

Reforma is Oaxaca's upscale residential neighborhood north of Centro. Tree-lined streets, good restaurants, and proximity to the Santo Domingo cultural area attract professionals and families seeking quality without tourist crowds.

Best for: Families, professionals, and nomads seeking quality residential living
✓ Pros
  • Quiet streets
  • Good restaurants
  • Near Santo Domingo
  • Safe
✗ Cons
  • Less character
  • Fewer tourists
  • Need transport

Xochimilco

$
Artisan & Traditional

Artisan neighborhood with workshop energy

Xochimilco is Oaxaca's artisan quarter - textile workshops, traditional crafts, and authentic neighborhood atmosphere. It's where Oaxaca's famous textiles are made, offering cultural immersion beyond the tourist center.

Best for: Craft lovers, culture seekers, and nomads interested in traditional Mexican artisanry
✓ Pros
  • Artisan workshops
  • Authentic culture
  • Affordable
  • Local markets
✗ Cons
  • Basic infrastructure
  • Less English
  • Far from cafes

San Felipe del Agua

$$$
Exclusive & Scenic

Mountain views in Oaxaca's exclusive heights

San Felipe del Agua climbs the hills north of Oaxaca - mountain views, cooler temperatures, and exclusive residences. It's where wealthy Oaxaqueños live, with growing expat presence seeking space and scenery.

Best for: Nature lovers, families, and nomads seeking views with exclusivity
✓ Pros
  • Mountain views
  • Cooler climate
  • Exclusive area
  • Space
✗ Cons
  • Need transport
  • Far from center
  • Limited walkability
