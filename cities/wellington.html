Best Neighborhoods in Wellington

Where to base yourself for the perfect nomad experience

Cuba Street

$$
Bohemian & Creative

Wellington's bohemian creative heart

Cuba Street is Wellington's soul - independent boutiques, diverse cafes, and creative energy. The bucket fountain is just the start of this pedestrian paradise.

Best for: Creatives, coffee lovers, and those seeking Wellington's soul
✓ Pros
  • Best cafes
  • Independent shops
  • Street performers
  • Creative energy
✗ Cons
  • Busy weekends
  • Limited parking
  • Hipster overload

Te Aro

$$
Entertainment & Central

Wellington's central entertainment zone

Te Aro surrounds Cuba Street and Courtenay Place - bars, restaurants, and Wellington's nightlife. It's flat, central, and where Wellington plays after dark.

Best for: Night owls, foodies, and those seeking central Wellington
✓ Pros
  • Best nightlife
  • Restaurant variety
  • Central location
  • Flat terrain
✗ Cons
  • Noisy nights
  • Expensive rent
  • Parking nightmare

Mount Victoria

$$$
Charming & Village

Wellington's charming hillside village

Mount Victoria is Wellington's prettiest neighborhood - wooden villas, harbor views, and village atmosphere. The lookout offers the best city views.

Best for: Long-term residents, view seekers, and those wanting village life
✓ Pros
  • Best views
  • Beautiful houses
  • Village feel
  • Near city
✗ Cons
  • Steep hills
  • Limited parking
  • Expensive

Thorndon

$$$
Historic & Political

Wellington's historic political quarter

Thorndon is Wellington's oldest suburb - historic buildings, Parliament, and Katherine Mansfield's birthplace. It's where Wellington's history and politics meet.

Best for: History lovers, political watchers, and those seeking heritage
✓ Pros
  • Historic architecture
  • Near Parliament
  • Botanic Gardens access
  • Quiet
✗ Cons
  • Limited restaurants
  • Quiet evenings
  • Expensive

Newtown

$
Diverse & Authentic

Wellington's diverse multicultural hub

Newtown is Wellington's most diverse suburb - ethnic restaurants, the zoo, and genuine multiculturalism. It's less polished but more authentic than the city center.

Best for: Foodies, budget travelers, and those seeking diversity
✓ Pros
  • Diverse food
  • Affordable
  • Zoo access
  • Authentic character
✗ Cons
  • Less pretty
  • Some rough areas
  • Far from waterfront
