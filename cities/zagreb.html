Best Neighborhoods in Zagreb

Where to base yourself for the perfect nomad experience

Gornji Grad (Upper Town)

$$
Historic & Atmospheric

Zagreb's historic hilltop heart

Gornji Grad is Zagreb's medieval core - St. Mark's Church, Stone Gate, and funicular access. It's atmospheric and the city's historic soul.

Best for: History lovers, photographers, and those seeking old Zagreb
✓ Pros
  • Historic sites
  • St. Mark's Church
  • Funicular
  • Views
✗ Cons
  • Steep access
  • Limited dining
  • Tourist focused

Donji Grad (Lower Town)

$$
Elegant & Cultural

Zagreb's grand Austro-Hungarian center

Donji Grad is Zagreb's elegant center - green horseshoe of parks, grand squares, and Austro-Hungarian architecture. It's Zagreb's sophisticated heart.

Best for: Architecture lovers, culture seekers, and those seeking refined Zagreb
✓ Pros
  • Grand architecture
  • Museums
  • Parks
  • Central location
✗ Cons
  • Busy streets
  • Less character
  • Can feel formal

Tkalčićeva

$$
Social & Vibrant

Zagreb's vibrant cafe and bar street

Tkalčićeva is Zagreb's social artery - cafes, bars, and street life connecting Upper and Lower Town. It's where Zagreb comes alive.

Best for: Social travelers, bar hoppers, and those seeking Zagreb's energy
✓ Pros
  • Best cafes
  • Vibrant atmosphere
  • Great bars
  • Central
✗ Cons
  • Touristy
  • Can be crowded
  • Noisy evenings

Maksimir

$
Green & Local

Zagreb's green eastern escape

Maksimir surrounds Zagreb's largest park - green spaces, local life, and stadium atmosphere. It's where Zagreb breathes.

Best for: Nature lovers, joggers, and those seeking local Zagreb
✓ Pros
  • Beautiful park
  • Zoo
  • Local atmosphere
  • Green space
✗ Cons
  • Far from center
  • Limited nightlife
  • Residential

Novi Zagreb

$
Local & Brutalist

Zagreb's socialist-era south

Novi Zagreb is Zagreb's Yugoslav-era expansion - brutalist architecture, Lake Jarun, and local residential life. It's different Zagreb.

Best for: Architecture fans, lake lovers, and those seeking alternative Zagreb
✓ Pros
  • Lake Jarun
  • Brutalist architecture
  • Affordable
  • Local feel
✗ Cons
  • Far from center
  • Less scenic
  • Limited tourism
