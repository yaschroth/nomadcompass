Best Neighborhoods in Manila

Where to base yourself for the perfect nomad experience

Makati

$$$
Business & Expat

Philippines' business capital with expat infrastructure

Makati is Manila's financial heart - gleaming towers, upscale malls, and the best international restaurants. Poblacion offers nightlife, while Legazpi Village hosts weekend markets. It's where most expats and digital nomads base themselves.

Best for: Business professionals, expats, and nomads seeking Manila's best infrastructure
✓ Pros
  • Best infrastructure
  • Great restaurants
  • Expat community
  • Safe
✗ Cons
  • Very expensive
  • Traffic nightmare
  • Corporate feel

BGC (Bonifacio Global City)

$$$
Modern & Planned

Manila's newest city with planned streets

BGC is Manila's modern showpiece - planned streets, pedestrian-friendly zones, and the newest malls and restaurants. It feels more Singapore than Manila, attracting young professionals and families seeking order in the chaos.

Best for: Families, professionals, and nomads wanting modern, walkable Manila
✓ Pros
  • Walkable streets
  • Modern everything
  • Very safe
  • Great dining
✗ Cons
  • Most expensive
  • Artificial feel
  • Far from culture

Poblacion, Makati

$$
Hip & Nightlife

Manila's hippest nightlife and food scene

Poblacion is Makati's old town turned nightlife hub - rooftop bars, trendy restaurants, and creative energy in historic streets. It's Manila's equivalent of Brooklyn, attracting young professionals and creative types.

Best for: Nightlife lovers, foodies, and nomads seeking Manila's trendiest scene
✓ Pros
  • Best nightlife
  • Trendy restaurants
  • Creative energy
  • Walkable
✗ Cons
  • Noisy nights
  • Limited accommodation
  • Party-focused

Ortigas

$$
Business & Practical

Manila's second CBD with value proposition

Ortigas is Manila's second business district - high-rises, malls, and corporate offices at more affordable prices than Makati. It lacks character but offers practical living with excellent shopping access.

Best for: Business travelers, budget nomads, and those seeking Makati alternative
✓ Pros
  • More affordable
  • Big malls
  • Business facilities
  • Good transport
✗ Cons
  • Less character
  • Traffic
  • Corporate feel

Quezon City

$
Local & Sprawling

Manila's largest city with local character

Quezon City is Manila's largest component city - universities, entertainment centers, and genuine Filipino neighborhood life. It's cheaper and more authentic than Makati, though less convenient for typical nomad needs.

Best for: Budget nomads, students, and those seeking authentic Filipino city life
✓ Pros
  • Affordable
  • Authentic
  • University energy
  • Entertainment
✗ Cons
  • Very spread out
  • Traffic
  • Less English
