Best Neighborhoods in Florence

Where to base yourself for the perfect nomad experience

Centro Storico

$$$
Renaissance & Tourist

Renaissance perfection in Florence's heart

Florence's historic center is Renaissance perfection - the Duomo, Uffizi, and Ponte Vecchio within walking distance. It's extremely touristy but incomparably beautiful. Stay here for short-term immersion in history.

Best for: Art lovers, first-time visitors, and nomads seeking iconic Florence
✓ Pros
  • Incredible art
  • Historic sites
  • Walkable
  • Beautiful
✗ Cons
  • Extremely touristy
  • Very expensive
  • Crowded

Oltrarno

$$
Artisan & Authentic

Artisan Florence on the other side of the river

Oltrarno is Florence's artisan quarter - across the Arno, away from worst crowds. Traditional workshops, local restaurants, and Piazza Santo Spirito create authentic atmosphere. Florence for those who stay longer.

Best for: Long-term nomads, craft lovers, and those seeking authentic Florence
✓ Pros
  • Artisan character
  • Less touristy
  • Local restaurants
  • Authentic
✗ Cons
  • Fewer sights
  • Basic infrastructure
  • Quieter

San Lorenzo

$$
Market & Central

Market life around Medici chapels

San Lorenzo surrounds the famous market and Medici Chapels. It's busy and commercial but central, with leather goods markets and food halls. Working Florence rather than postcard Florence.

Best for: Market lovers, food enthusiasts, and budget nomads seeking central location
✓ Pros
  • Famous market
  • Central location
  • Food options
  • Affordable
✗ Cons
  • Very busy
  • Tourist vendors
  • Noisy

Santa Croce

$$
Local & Central

Florentine neighborhood life near famous basilica

Santa Croce offers more neighborhood feel - the famous basilica, Piazza Santa Croce, and local life just east of center. It's where Florentines actually live while staying close to everything.

Best for: Long-term residents, sports fans (stadium nearby), and those seeking local life
✓ Pros
  • Neighborhood feel
  • Great square
  • Near center
  • More local
✗ Cons
  • Game day crowds
  • Some tourist overflow
  • Rising prices

Campo di Marte

$
Residential & Local

Residential Florence near the stadium

Campo di Marte is Florence's residential east - near the stadium, with parks and modern infrastructure. It's where Florentine families live, offering value and authentic life away from tourist center.

Best for: Families, long-term nomads, and those seeking authentic residential life
✓ Pros
  • Good value
  • Local life
  • Parks
  • Train station
✗ Cons
  • Far from center
  • Less charming
  • Game day chaos
